Applying for Early Admission to First Grade

First grade early admission is based on rigorous academic standards

The State of Maryland has determined that six years of age is an appropriate age to begin first grade. In many cases, children admitted early to school will be working in a setting with older children. To develop a positive perspective about the school experience, children should feel comfortable, competent, and confident as they learn in the classroom setting. Therefore, only children who meet rigorous admission standards will be accepted early into first grade.

Requirements

Age: sixth birthday falls between September 2 and October 13

Standard Requirements

  • Early admission applicants must pass an assessment at their school by designated school staff in a one-on-one setting.
  • Applications require completion of developmental checklists.

Admission determined by: child's prospective school

Interested in early admission? Find out more about applying ↓

Testing Requirement

Early admission applicants will be assessed at their prospective school by designated staff in a one-on-one setting between May and July. Curriculum-based assessment tools will be used to measure their abilities in reading and mathematics in order to ensure their readiness for first grade.

Children do not need any type of special preparation for the test. Children who already possess the needed skills will have an opportunity to show them during the assessment.

Early Admission Factors

As stated in the Howard County Public School System Policy 9000 (Student Residency, Eligibility, Enrollment and Assignment), children accepted for early admission must demonstrate a high level of academic, social, emotional, and physical development.

An assessment of academic skills, as well as a developmental checklist of non-academic competencies, are primary factors for early admission.

Notification of Decision

Parents/guardians will be notified by letter from their prospective school regarding the decision of acceptance or denial for early admission. These letters will be mailed following their assessment.

Applying for Early Admission

Applications about early admission to first grade for school year 2017-2018 will be posted in February 27, 2017.

A complete application includes the following:

  • Application for Early Admission to First Grade
  • Developmental Checklist A (For Children Being Considered for Early Admission to School): This form is to be completed by the child’s Kindergarten teacher or an adult other than the parent/guardian or family member. It is the responsibility of the parent/guardian to follow up with the person asked to submit Developmental Checklist A.
  • Developmental Checklist B (For Children Being Considered for Early Admission to School): This form is to be submitted by the child’s parent(s) or guardian.
  • Documentation of successful completion of an approved kindergarten experience

Application Timeline

Step 1: March – May

Parents submit all required documentation to their prospective school.

Step 2: between April – June*

*Soon after receipt of application packet.

Parent(s)/Guardian(s) are contacted by school regarding assessment appointment.

Step 3: between May – July*

*Soon after receipt of application packet.

Children are assessed at prospective school.

Step 4: After assessment decisions

Parents/guardians will be notified by letter from their prospective school regarding the decision of acceptance or denial for early admission. These letters will be mailed following their assessment.
