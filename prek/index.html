Applying for Early Admission to Pre-K

Pre-K early admission is based on need

The HCPSS Pre-K program is provided, per Maryland State Department of Education (MSDE) regulations, to any child who meets first priority criteria (disadvantaged economic status, homelessness, and foster care). This eligibility is based on information supplied in Section III, 13a. or 13b. of the HCPSS Pre-K Early Admission application.

Requirements

Age: fourth birthday falls between September 2 and October 13

Demonstrated need requirement:

  • The student meets first priority criteria (disadvantaged economic status, homelessness, and foster care)
  • In addition, students must demonstrate a need for early intervention as defined by at-risk developmental factors that critically impact potential success in kindergarten (Policy 9000)

Admission determined by: Office of Early Childhood Programs and Office of Early Intervention Services

Meet the requirements? Find out more about applying ↓

At-Risk Development Factors

As stated in the Howard County Public School System Policy 9000 (Student Residency, Eligibility, Enrollment and Assignment), children accepted for early admission to HCPSS Pre-K must demonstrate a need for early intervention as defined by at-risk developmental factors that critically impact potential success in kindergarten.

Early admission does not guarantee that the child will be promoted to the next grade. Decisions regarding promotion of early admitted Pre-K students to kindergarten are based on performance during the Pre-K year.

Notification of Decision

After reviewing submitted application documents, the Office of Early Childhood Programs will notify parents/guardians of qualification or non-qualification based on income eligibility and based on demonstrated need for early intervention.

A decision of acceptance or denial for Early Admission to HCPSS Pre-K will be mailed, with instructions for next steps. Eligible students may have to wait for placement, which is dependent upon available space at the start of the school year.

Applying for Early Admission

Applications about early admission to Pre-K for school year 2017-2018 will be posted in February 27, 2017.

A complete application for Early Admission to Pre-K includes the following:

  • Application for Early Admission to Pre-K.
  • Ages & Stages Questionnaire FORM A: this form is to be submitted by the child’s parent(s) or guardian in advance of the July 29, 2017 deadline.
  • Ages & Stages Questionnaire FORM B: this form is to be completed by the child’s preschool teacher or an adult other than the parent/guardian or family member. It is the responsibility of the parent/guardian to follow up with the person asked to submit Ages & Stage Questionnaire FORM B in advance of the July 29, 2017 deadline.

Application Timeline

Step 1: March 1 - July 29

Parent(s)/guardian(s) submit Application and Ages & Stages Questionnaire (Form A) to the HCPSS Office of Early Childhood Programs.

Parent(s)/guardian(s) request that preschool teacher, child care provider, or other non-related adult complete an Ages and Stages Questionnaire (Form B) and submit it to the Office of Early Childhood Programs.

* It is the responsibility of the parent/guardian to follow up with the person asked to complete and mail Ages & Stages Questionnaire FORM B in advance of the July 29, 2017 deadline.

Step 2: March – August*

*Soon after receipt of application packet.

During this time, qualified students are assessed.

Parent(s)/guardian(s) receive notification from the Office of Early Childhood Programs which will include status of:

  • qualification or non-qualification for disadvantaged economic status
  • qualification or non-qualification for early admission assessment
  • assessment appointment, if applicable

Step 3: No later than August 12, 2016

Parent(s)/guardian(s) receive notification of acceptance or denial for early admission.

Step 4: August – September 30, 2016

Eligible students may have to wait for placement, which is dependent upon available space at the start of the school year.

If accepted, parent(s)/guardian(s) register children at assigned school. Find out more about student registration →

If denied, parent(s)/guardian(s) may appeal decision of early admission.
