Applying for Early Admission to First Grade

First grade early admission is based on rigorous academic standards

The State of Maryland has determined that five years of age is an appropriate age to begin kindergarten. In many cases, children admitted early to school will be working in a setting with older children. To develop a positive perspective about the school experience, children should feel comfortable, competent, and confident as they learn in the classroom setting. Therefore, only children who meet rigorous admission standards will be accepted early into the kindergarten program.

Requirements

Age: sixth birthday falls between September 2 and October 13

Determined by: child's prospective school based on rigorous admission standards.

Standard Requirements

  • Applications require a developmental checklist completed by a kindergarten teacher.
  • Early admission applicants must pass an assessment at their school by designated school staff in a one-on-one setting.

Testing Requirement

Early admission applicants will be assessed at their prospective school by designated staff in a one-on-one setting between May and July. Curriculum based assessment tools will be used to measure their abilities in reading and mathematics in order to ensure their readiness for first grade.

Children do not need any type of special preparation for the test. Children who already possess the needed skills will have an opportunity to show them during the assessment.

Early Admission Factors

As stated in the Howard County Public School System Policy 9000 (Student Residency, Eligibility, Enrollment and Assignment), children accepted for early admission must demonstrate a high level of academic, social, emotional, and physical development.

An assessment of academic skills, as well as a developmental checklist of non-academic competencies, are primary factors for early admission.

Notification of Decision

Parents/guardians will be notified by letter from the Office of Early Childhood Programs regarding the decision of acceptance or denial for early admission. These letters will be mailed following their assessment.

Applying for Early Admission

A complete application includes the following:

  • Application for Early Admission to First Grade
  • Developmental Checklist A (For Children Being Considered for Early Admission to School) This form is to be completed by the child’s Kindergarten teacher or an adult other than the parent/guardian or family member. It is the responsibility of the parent/guardian to follow up with the person asked to complete and mail Development Checklist A in advance of the deadline.
  • Developmental Checklist B (For Children Being Considered for Early Admission to School) This form is to be completed by the child’s parent(s) or guardian, and mailed to the address at the bottom of the form.
  • Documentation about approved kindergarten experience.

Application Timeline

Step 1: March – May

Parent(s)/Guardian(s) submit Application and Developmental Checklist B to the their designated school.

Parent(s)/Guardian(s) complete top section of Developmental Checklist A and request that kindergarten teacher complete the remainder of the form and submit to designated school.

Step 2: between April – June (but soon after applications are received)

Parent(s)/Guardian(s) are contacted by school regarding assessment appointment

Step 3: between May – July (but soon after applications are received)

Children are assessed at designated school.

Step 4: After assessment decisions

Parent(s)/Guardian(s) receive notification via phone calls and/or letters regarding acceptance or denial of application from designated school.

Students can be registered as soon as possible after receipt of acceptance letter for early admission

Submitting Appeals

Upon receipt of denial for early admission, parent(s)/guardian(s) may appeal decision of early admission.

All written appeals should be submitted to the Office of Early Childhood Education, earlyadmission@hcpss.org
